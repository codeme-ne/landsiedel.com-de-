Defence mechanism

A term from psychoanalysis. It refers to certain psychological behavior patterns, which serve to mitigate and eliminate internal conflict. For example: suppression/replacement, regression, denial, projection.

One behaves as if something was true that was not yet true. It helps to overcome a blockage, fears, etc.

One is transferred into the past by means of suggestions, hypnosis or the like and experiences it as real as possible. It may be that one shows temporary childish behavior patterns and can no longer rely on all of its adult abilities.

Ambiguity

(Ambiguity ) means that a word or phrase can have several meanings. This forces the coachee to search for the meanings appropriate to the context. There are different types of ambiguity:

  1. Phonological: In the same sentence, use two words with different meanings that sound similar; or use one of them in a context in which it could also be thought of as the other. Consider this more/sea of possibilities. The possibilities you see/sow now will increase many times.
  2. Syntactical: Use a sequence of words that can have multiple meanings. There are endless solutions, they are waiting for them. They are visiting relative. ["You are visiting relatives." or "There are relatives who are visiting."]
  3. Field ambiguity: Use of a more precise definition in a linguistic context in which it remains unclear which other part of the sentence refers to the same. If I speak to you as a child ... (Who is the child?)
  4. Interpunctuation ambiguity:
    1. Into each other-transition of two sentences: Use of the last word or part of the sentence in one sentence as the first word or part of the sentence in the next sentence. "I want to notice your hand me the glass." [German example: "I want you to give me the hand to give me the glass."] Our time today will surely pass me the glass. You will slowly close your eyes to this chapter.
    2. Unexpected interruption: As you read this .... book, you will learn new techniques.
    3. Incomplete sentence: I know you're expecting.
