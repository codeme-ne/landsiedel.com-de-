NLP is not unscientific

From the side of those who intend to formulate substantive criticism of NLP, it is usually heard that NLP is unscientific.

The allegation that NLP is unscientific usually comes from persons who are themselves academics (and mostly also themselves are involved in the psychological, therapeutic or coaching field). NLP The predecessors and founders were and are mostly scientists, but the concept of NLP was aimed at collecting and making the most efficient communication and treatment methods available to the general public. This eclectic approach led to the inclusion in the NLP-Fundus of those methods which are not part of the fundus of psychologists and psychotherapists – it is believed that certain methods of NLP are unscientific in the sense of "not coming from science".

In general, however, NLP is not the hokuspocus to which its opponents make it. The basis of the NLP philosophy is constructivism, a recognized social science school of thought that starts from the subjectivity of each perception. Likewise, many methods from other humanist therapy schools were made usable (e.g. family therapy, gestalt therapy, etc.).

However, one reason for this reputation "NLP = unscientific" is ultimately also due to the handling of certain user groups with the subject matter of the debate. Not a few NLP providers have their own profit in mind instead of training, others water down training standards or refuse to do so completely. In addition, bridges from NLP to other, parascientific or esoteric-oriented fields are struck from the margins. Thus, one can certainly call these areas of overlap to NLP unscientific.

On the other hand, there is also academic recognition, for example, there are own NLP courses that are affiliated to psychology; in Austria, neurolinguistic psychotherapy (NLPt) is officially recognized as a psychotherapeutic method. In summary, NLP is not a science, rather a sub-area of psychology. However, just as NLP represents its own science, it can be said that NLP is unscientific.

NLP and Science – A Collection

On this page we would like to present our collection of summarized studies. Some of the studies were carried out by trainers from our ranks, others are many years old. In this project we have already invested many hundred hours of work. First we wanted to examine all the studies, but the time spent on procurement was enormous. Therefore, we started to collect some studies and summarize them, which prove the effectiveness of NLP. In a second step we plan to take some of the studies apart, which have been used as evidence against the effectiveness of NLP so far. In doing so, we often made hairy mistakes in the research design, such as in the studies on eye access instructions.

Newer findings from brain research prove the effectiveness of NLP more and more. Therefore, it actually uses us that the critics still trust the press with age-old and poorly done studies. With this page, we also want to call on serious researchers to send us their results and not simply to claim that there is a study somewhere that would refute NLP, but unfortunately this study cannot be made available to us. This does not correspond to the scientific procedure.

Ärzte mit NLP-Practitioner gehen besser auf ihre Patienten ein!

Stephanie Bohn publishes a dissertation in which she examines 21 doctors who took part in an NLP-practitioner training, among other things in relation to empathy in the doctor-patient conversation. In her qualitative-quantitative method mix, she concluded that the NLP training has a positive effect on the physician-patient relationship.

Visuell-Kinästhetische Dissoziation in Studie erprobt

At the University of Alberta in Canada an NLP treatment form was tested in 1987. In a very small sample of two women who were abused, trance, anchoring and especially visual-kinesthetic dissociation (also known as phobia technique) were tested. Both women showed a marked improvement, which continued three weeks after treatment.

Jaap Hollander, 20 days before and after an NLP coaching session, collected data from 25 subjects who had small psychological or social problems. 16 of the subjects (64%) felt significantly better after coaching by an NLP practitioner who was still in training.

Methodische Fehler in NLP-Studien

The question of which methodological errors arise in NLP studies was raised by Eric Opposition and Bruce Forman in 1985. In their meta-analysis of all NLP studies (a total of 39) that became known until 1984, they identified 6 categories of errors.

NLP bei der Behandlung von Klaustrophobie

MRI (magnet resonance imaging) studies often cause problems with claustrophobic people, as it is best not to move during the MRI. People who are afraid of closed spaces are now moving because of stress or do not even go to an examination. Apart from an induced anesthesia, there is the possibility of an NLP treatment that was investigated more closely in England in 2010.
