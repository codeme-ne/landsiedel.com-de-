What is NLP? NLP simply explained

Reading time: 15 minutes

Neurolinguistic programming, NLP for short, is a motivational and communication model developed in the seventies. It was developed by the analysis of very successful psychotherapists and is now used in a wide range of areas of life, such as therapy, sales, leadership, partnership and dealing with children.

The NLP brings together conversational, behavioral, hypno- and body-oriented approaches. Our thinking, feeling and behaviour (neuro) is systematically changed (programmed) by means of language (linguistics). Over the past 25 years, our trainer team has carried out nearly 20,000 training days. Our managing director is Germany President of the world's largest NLP association.

Where does NLP come from?

Richard Bandler, John Grinder and Frank Pucelik John Grinder was an assistant professor of linguistics at the University of California in Santa Cruz at that time. Richard Bandler was a mathematician, gestalt therapist and computer specialist. Together with Frank Pucelik, they wanted to learn and develop.

They found out how experts achieved very special achievements in their fields. Thus, John Grinder, Richard Bandler and Frank Pucelik began successful people Modeling and the patterns they used to achieve their goals, for example, they looked into the question of why some well-known psychotherapists had so much success in treating their clients and others didn't. They realized that their work had to be based on a common pattern of behaviour.

Bandler, Grinder and Pucelik developed a model that is applicable to everyone and promotes communication, learning and personal change. They called it "Neurolinguistic programming". Bandler, Grinder and Pucelik passed on their knowledge to students in workshops and seminars. They became famous mainly by modeling hypnotherapist Dr. Milton Erickson, Family Therapist Virgina Satir and Gestalttherapist Fritz Perls.

Is NLP dangerous?

Attention! NLP is dangerous if the user has not completed a professional training which explains which problems may occur in certain types of treatment. If the implementation is incorrect, the user can: Trance inductions, confronting therapeutic measures and trauma treatment in the client cause long-term psychological damage.

Many interventions must not be carried out at all if there is no justification as a medical practitioner or psychotherapist. Professional NLP trainers know exactly how far their competences reach and where their limits lie in the application.

On the other hand, NLP can overstepped Self-esteem and omnipotence. Therefore, users may believe that they are invulnerable through knowledge. Completely free from the burden of the ability to self-criticism and through self-confirmation, users can lose the ground.

Why NLP is criticized is often due to bias, ignorance, premature associations and negative individual experiences of the individual. Although the roots of the method are anchored in science and there are many studies that speak for their effectiveness, there are nevertheless critics who claim that NLP is non-scientifically based Enough.

Others believe that reading thoughts with the help of NLP and manipulate people can. NLP is a danger! An insight into the techniques opens up many new possibilities, which can lead, among other things, to direct the behavior of a person in a specific direction. The knowledge about oneself and the others, and a deepened human knowledge, can be called manipulation.

Also one should face the promises of loud marketers with skepticism. "NLP leads in the blink of an eye to Happiness, wealth and success" is a popular sentence that you should not believe too much. It is not that simple in reality after all.

The subconscious plays an important role for decisions and actions. Richard Bandler and John Grinder assumed this in the 1970s. They take into account in their work that the unconscious has to be involved in profound change work. Only if the brain is taken into account can the work of change prove to be really sustainable and effective.

The NLP assumptions


The map is not the area.
We all have different conceptions of the world, but none of these conceptions represents the world completely and accurately. People do not react to reality itself, but to their own image of reality.

There are no mistakes – just feedback.
Each reaction and event can be used as feedback and as a way to learn.

There are no real or false models in the world.
To do certain tasks, however, some models are more useful than others. If you want to do a hike with an Autoatlas, you do not use such a useful model. Simply the hike with a hiking map would be easy. Nevertheless, the Autoatlas is not wrong.

Everyone has all the resources in them.
In the NLP, we assume that people are full of resources. Sometimes, however, they don't have access to it. Then, an experienced programmer helps to restore access. Through appropriate resources, people can solve problems and do amazing things.

People make the best choice within their model to the world.
If people have other and more appropriate opportunities for meeting their needs, they would not do much of what sometimes seems to happen out of pure wickedness.

Behind every even so problematic behavior/symptom is a good intention.
In the life of the person, each behavior aims at a positive function, regardless of possible negative side effects.

For each behavior there is a context in which it can be useful or useful.
It is learned, that is, that the behavior eventually leads to the desired goal. The aim is also to develop more options in addition to this behavior.

Resistance with the client means lack of flexibility of the consultant.
Resistance does not result from malice of the opponent, but is an indication of missing relationship. So the consultant can himself do something to ensure that the connection to the client is correct.

If something doesn't work, do something else.
All too often we expect that we only have to try it long enough. But if you run against the wall, it can take quite a long time for you to get through it. It would be good to try another approach and, for example, look for a door.

The Associations

NLP Institutes: The International Association of NLP Institutes is the world's largest NLP association with more than 10,000 members. For each country there is a separate president who supervises the master trainers and takes care of the development and compliance with the high standards in terms of quality, professionalism and ethics. The association organizes world congresses and has own projects that advance NLP in the world. The certificate of this association is with us by default.

DVNLP: The German Association for Neuro-Linguistic Programming e.V. is a large expert forum for people who use NLP in the professional and private context. The association sets up curricula and controls the observance of the rules by the instructors. Once a year a congress and the one event called Future Tools take place.

INLPTA: The International NLP Trainers Association is an international, global NLP Trainer Association that takes care of quality, professionalism, ethics and business applications in the NLP. It is represented and associated with its own coordinators in China, Japan, USA, Australia, New Zealand and in most European countries.

What does NLP have to do with psychology?

When in the 19th and 20th centuries psychology dealt almost exclusively with the topics of depression and mental illness, the American psychologist Martin Seligman to reverse the view and consciously positive Feelings He found that optimism can be learned and that he wanted to promote the strengths of others.

Positive Psychology found out that you can increase your well-being and happiness by using your strength of character. If a person pursues an activity that makes sense to him and uses his strengths in doing so, he lives a much healthier life. Happy people live longer and are not so susceptible to mental illness.

Martin Seligman distinguished six virtues to which 24 character strengths are assigned:

  • Wisdom and knowledge: creativity, curiosity, openness, joy of learning, perspective
  • Courage: Courage, Perseverance, Integrity, Vitality
  • Humanity: love, kindness, social intelligence
  • Justice: social responsibility, fairness, leadership
  • Moderation: Forgiveness and compassion, humility, modesty, prudence, self-regulation
  • Transcendence: Appreciation of beauty and excellence, gratitude, hope, humor, spirituality.

NLP in psychotherapy and counselling

Historically, NLP has developed its primordial therapy in various short-term therapeutic, solution-oriented, hypno-systemic and constructivist psychotherapy approaches. In recent years, NLP psychotherapy has been invented – an independent psychotherapy approach, which, however, strives for professional recognition. In some practices, psychotherapists offer solution-oriented short-term therapies and use NLP specifically. Apart from this, there are overlaps in the treatment methods of classical psychotherapy and neurolinguistic programming. The most well-known is probably the treatment of phobias with NLP. It can help people overcome phobia within a very short time.

NLP offers tools...

  • to establish trusting relationships with different clients,
  • to understand the holistic structure and effect of attitudes, beliefs, values and self-images,
  • an extended understanding of the effect of language,
  • for process-oriented work, which protects both the integrity and privacy of clients, as well as facilitates permanent changes,
  • to the solution-oriented and short-term therapeutically oriented setting.

NLP, Education and School

The earlier children learn to communicate, the better. Of course, it also depends on what social environment they are in and what they look at from others. NLP basically promotes communication between parents and children. Adults discover how they can communicate with their children in a positive, understanding way.

When parents express themselves clearly, they enable their child to understand them and react appropriately. When it comes to communication, the choice of words is particularly important. Constant denials can have a negative effect on the child's behaviour. "Don't lick" may lead to more clacking, as the child, in order to follow this command, must first think about the action.

Those who want to understand children should move into the world of the child. Only in this way can it be ensured that the message arrives and is understood at all.

  • What does my child already know?
  • How's it feeling right now?
  • Do I understand?

NLP can also be helpful in the school system, where certain learning strategies are helpful to assert themselves and withstand the pressure of performance. Self-confidence learning can be fun and even healthy!
