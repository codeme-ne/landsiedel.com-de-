NLP Introduction and Overview

NLP is a modern communication tool and a tool for changing one's personality. It can be used in the therapeutic field as well as in business and many other contexts. Here you will get an overview of the NLP content, basic assumptions, founders, history, learning stages and definitions of NLP.

What does NLP mean?

NLP stands for neurolinguistic programming. Here the three components of the name are explained:

Neuro, linguistic, programming.

Neuro

Each behavior is controlled by neuronal processes. Our perception takes place through our five senses. The information is transmitted through our nervous system in our body. Our nervous system mainly includes our brain and spinal cord. From there, our thinking, feeling and behavior are controlled.

Linguistic

We communicate with other people through our language. At the same time, communication also takes place within ourselves. So we have an external and an internal communication.

Programming

Our learned behavioral and mentalities can be changed by targeted intervention. We can program for our desired behavior. Changes do not have to be left to chance, but can be done systematically.

Definitions of NLP

Here you will find classic definitions as well as simplistic definitions that give you a first idea of what NLP is.

NLP stands for neurolinguistic programming and is a communication and motivation model developed in the USA in the 1980s. It was developed by the linguistic professor John Grinder and the mathematician and gestalt therapist Richard Bandler by modeling outstanding therapists and can be applied in its present form in almost every area of life - leadership, therapy, sports, health, sales, rhetoric, relationship.

NLP offers a variety of methods to make your feelings, behavioral and mental patterns aware and to develop goal-oriented and constructively. In addition, NLP also offers many ways to discover and develop personal strengths and abilities.

NLP understands itself as a method by which one can learn to become aware of one's own and foreign modes of perception, expression and automatic patterns of thought and behaviour and to expand or change them (with the help of the inner, partly unconscious potential). Behind the term lies a long-standing research on the structure of subjective experiences (how people perceive and react to the world), communication (how people express and communicate) and behaviour patterns (how people store and automatically repeat what they have learned). Strategies successful people, who are described in detail and thus made accessible and learningable to anyone who wants to. The findings and contents of the NLP contribute to improving communication between people and offer a variety of techniques for finding goals and solutions in case of problems.

NLP is the art and science of personal perfection, efficient communication and excellence. "Art" is because everyone brings his unique personality and style into what he does, and this can never be grasped in words or techniques. "Science" is because there is a method and a method to discover the patterns that are used by outstanding individuals in all possible areas to achieve excellent results. This method is called modeling, "modeling" or modeling, and the patterns, skills and techniques that have been discovered by it are increasingly used in counseling, education and in the business world for more effective communication, personal growth and accelerated learning. NLP is a practical skill that brings us the results that we really want to achieve in this world, while at the same time creating something valuable for others. It is researching what makes the difference between the outstanding and the average.

History of the NLP

On this page you will find a brief account of the history of the NLP, who the people involved were and how NLP developed. A detailed account can be found on the page History of the NLP.

The history of the NLP begins in the seventies of the twentieth century, when the two American scientists Richard Bandler and John Grinder met. John Grinder was at that time assistant professor of linguistics at the University of California in Santa Cruz, USA. The two decided to find out how experts achieve very special achievements in certain areas, while others fail. These successful people took them as models and analyzed the patterns that these people use to achieve their desired goals.

Grinder and Bandler have become famous especially by the Modeling of four very fascinating personalities: Dr. Milton Erickson, probably the most important hypnotherapist in the world, Virginia Satir, an exceptional family therapist, Fritz Perls, the father of Gestalttherapy and Gregory Bateson, an incredibly intellectual and versatile philosopher and anthropologist.

After recognizing the patterns of these communication masters, they began to pass them on and develop them to students in workshops and seminars.

Meanwhile, NLP has spread throughout the world and is used in numerous areas such as therapy, relationship, sales, sports and education.

Model of learning stages

The model of the learning stages describes in which development steps our learning takes place.


Unconscious incompetence
You've never thought about anything before, and therefore you don't realize that you can't.		For example, a person is driving in the car, but the question of his own driving licence has not yet arisen.
Conscious incompetence
Recognizing that you can't do something (that is, you have to learn and practice if you want to do it).		For example, you would like to take the driver's license and sit in a car at the wheel for the first time.
Conscious competence
Conscious appropriation and application of the newly learned skills.		For example, when driving, you still have to concentrate fully and consciously pay attention to everything that needs to be done.
Unconscious competence
Automatic and unconscious application of new skills.		e.g. driving is so self-evident that you can talk at the same time.

NLP anticipations

In the NLP some useful assumptions were formulated. These are not objective facts, but they facilitate communication with others and one's own life. NLP anticipations.

Working with beliefs

Success blockades lie only too often in ourselves. Sometimes they are completely unconscious to us, but often they also appear in the form of sentences that go through our minds when we start a project or get an order. Beliefs like: "I'm too young/old. I don't have money. I don't have enough education/experience. I don't have good relationships. I come from simple relationships. I don't deserve it. I'm not worth it" etc. Our beliefs form the backbone of our reality constructions. Learn what influence these sentences have on your life, how they originated and how you can change them in yourself and others. Train yourself to create more useful beliefs. You can use numerous techniques and methods, including: Sleight-of-Mouth-patterns, Dickens Pattern, New Behavior Generator and many more.

Working with values

Values like freedom, love, honesty or success are our inner motivators. They ultimately determine and direct our actions. Because of them, wars are waged, horrific torments are endured and people are destroyed. Nevertheless, most people are not even aware of their own values and even less the values of their partners, friends and customers. We often get into conflict if we do not have a clear order in our values and ask our highest values for opposite actions, e.g. in the question of career or/and family. You learn in this block how values arise and how you can find out your personal hierarchy of values. You deal with your conflicts of values and rules of life and train yourself to find out in a conversation the values and rules of life of your partner or your colleague. If you act in harmony with your values, then you will feel a new, strong energy in yourself.

Advanced hypnosis techniques

Advanced methods of clinical hypnosis are trained: trance inductions and trance phenomena, post-hypnotic orders, ideomotor signals, self-hypnosis, advanced methods of development of Metaphors as well as the use of trance elements in everyday communication processes.

Meta-programs

Meta-programs are person-specific perception filters that become visible in the behavior of a person. They edit, shape and design those information from the outside world that we allow to get inside. Learn to recognize and use these programs with you and others.

Advanced lifestyle

With the help of further time-line methods, you will continue to design your life, edit traumas and shape your future.

Modeling

Learn how to reproduce human excellence at an advanced level. This block closes the circle of NLP beginnings to today's applications. You learn to find out the patterns of successful people and make them usable for yourself and others.
