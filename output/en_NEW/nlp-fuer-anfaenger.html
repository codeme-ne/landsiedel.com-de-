5 powerful NLP techniques

NLP offers effective methods in various fields of application. They serve to improve communication and the respective areas of life. All techniques are fundamentally effective, you only have to implement them. Techniques in everyday life and improve your perception.

  1. Repeat

    Have you ever had a conversation with a person who made you feel super? You felt you understood and you were on a wavelength. When this happens, you talk about Repeat (Rapport = equality). You can create a relationship between you and your partner in order to create sympathy and trust. By the way, you can also increase your willingness to buy or make a price discount easier.

    But not only in sales, the ability to make rapport to its counterpart is very important, but also in communication, in discussions and conflict solutions, you get to your goal the fastest when you first build rapport.

    In order to successfully produce rapport to another person, two further NLP methods are needed: the so-called "Pacing and the "Leading.

    Read on here to find out!

  2. Anchors

    Under the heading: "Nearby" One understands a stimulus (starter, stimulus, trigger), which triggers a certain, always the same reaction in a human being. This means that in some situations we subconsciously anchor, by combining a feeling (joyful/tough, anxious/courage, etc.) with a situation. In doing so, both positive and negative feelings can be anchored. In order not to be victims of negative associations, we can use the powerful NLP technique of anchoring for us. here Read more.

    Exercise: Put yourself in a situation where you have laughed a lot. Take the feeling of amusement very well and anchor just before the climax of your feeling – for example, stick your hand to a fist and squeeze three times. Then pull off briefly and re-test the anchor by pressing your handballs again. Now you should feel amused again.

    Read on here to find out!

  3. Representation systems: VAKOG

    Man experiences the world through his sensory organs. In the NLP we distinguish five different systems of representation:

    • VIcelandic (see/eyes)
    • Auditative (hearing/ears)
    • Kinesthetic (feeling/skin)
    • Olfactoric (smelling/nase)
    • GOstatoric (flavouring/tongue)

    Each person tends more or less towards some perceptions, so you can type people (visual, auditory, kinesthetic, olfactory, gustatoric). If you know which type your interlocutor is, you can make rapport even easier and faster.

    Read on here to find out!

  4. Swish technique

    With the Swish technique can dissolve or change bad habits and build strong motivation. These are images that we project in our head in front of our mental eye.

    Exercise: Imagine an image of your unwanted behavior (e.g. chewing nails) and create an additional target image of how you want it instead. Then you exchange both images with the help of the SWISH TECHNIQUE ==References==

    Read on here to find out!

  5. Meta-model

    With the Meta-model to the communication champion: When speaking, repayments, generalizations and distortions are often used, whereby statements are often misinterpreted and conflicts arise.

    With the meta-model, you penetrate into the depth structure of what you have said, clarify ambiguities and avoid misunderstandings.
