Get to know and apply NLP techniques and methods

NLP techniques are available in a variety of designs, due to the large number of NLP applications. They can be addressed to individuals, but also to couples and whole groups. While some NLP techniques require a few minutes, others require much more time.

One focus is the training of perception. Perception is a prerequisite for successful communication: only who sees what triggers their own words and the body language can react accordingly and act according to needs. With corresponding language models, language patterns and so-called Representation systems a deeper contact to the interlocutor can be established.

There are techniques that give you the ability to adapt your feelings to the needs of a situation – indispensable not only for better well-being in yourself, but also for adequate communication with the interlocutors. In addition, techniques with which the way we perceive our reality can be changed in order to reconstruct this. In this way, new meanings can be added to various situations, so as to find new possibilities for dealing with them.

A certain variety of NLP techniques are the strategies that control and control human behavior. In NLP methods have been developed with which Strategies for example, to optimize creativity, flexibility, motivation and decision-making processes. At an advanced level, it is learned how to replicate human excellence: one finds out the patterns of successful people to make them usable for oneself.

Calibration

Calibration the ability to recognize externally perceptible features and to assign them to the state. So, for example, after I set myself well to my counterpart, I can see on the basis of a fine change in the face whether a proposal is good or whether further arguments may be necessary.

Calibration is not pacing. Nevertheless, a fine perception, as is necessary for calibration, is an important prerequisite for pacing. Calibration initially only means that I take information from the voice, face or other body reactions. Whether I mirror this (pace) is another thing.

Exercise to two:

  • Person A denkt an eine Person die sie gerne mag. Person B achtet auf die Physiology the person A.
  • Danach denkt Person A an eine Person die sie nicht mag. Person B achtet wieder auf die Physiologie der Person.
  • After that, Person B asks Person A questions and tries to find out which person it is. Questions are “What person has more temperament?”, “What person lives further away?”, What person ...?”

Mirrors

Mirrors denotes physical fit to posture, gesture, breathing, mimic, movements or weight shifts, muscle tone etc. Here you adapt like a mirror to everything you can see.

Practice:
Spiegle the body movements of other people and thus build rapport to them.

Pacing

Pacing means adapting to the other in the entire visual and auditory expression behavior. The other person is picked up where she stands. For example, I like to pace the pace of speech, the rhythm, the tone of the other. This category also includes everything that belongs to mirroring.

Practice:
Call a friend and pace on the phone his speech speed and volume. Also pay attention to his favorite formulations.

Rapport

When people meet each other, their expression behavior usually adapts consciously or unconsciously to each other. The more positive they see contact, the stronger their similarities in communication. This effect can be reversed by reflecting the behavior of the opposite and thus Rapport (a positive interpersonal relationship). Rapport can be produced with “mirror” and “Pacing”.

Practice:
Watch on the street, restaurants, public events and wherever you are in the next time, whether there is a repeat between the people involved or not.

Anchor techniques

The Anchors is a conscious conditioning of a stimulus with a reaction, whereby feelings can be stored in order to call them again later. This allows you to put yourself into a resource-rich state, for example, in order to better manage tasks.

An anchor can be set:
First, determine the resource, the state you would like to have available at the touch of a button and select the anchor (can be a pressure on a body) with which you want to retrieve this state.

  • Remember fully associated with an experience in which you had the desired resource available.
  • Press the selected anchor shortly before the climax, i.e. the highest intensity of the resourceful state.
  • Separator: interrupt the state, e.g. with a distracting question.
  • Test: Restart anchor to test whether it works.

Representation systems

Representation systems describing how we perceive the world through the five senses, where each person sets a different focus (VAKOG = visual, auditory, kinetic, olfactory, gustatory). One of the typical representation systems is the VAKOG model.

VAKOG

After the NLP model VAKOG people use their own five sensory channels with different focus.

  • V: Visual – See
  • A: Auditive – Listening
  • K: Kinästhetisch – Beühren/Fühlen
  • O: Olfactory – smell
  • G: Gustatoric – Taste

Practice:
Listen to your favorite songs and identify the different sense-specific words.

Eye movement patterns

Short, unconscious movements of the eyes in certain directions give insight into what is happening in the opposite. In general, it can be said that left information is remembered and right information is constructed. Visual and auditory memories are accordingly left and visual and auditory designs are right. In addition, the retrieval of feelings (eyes go down) and the inner dialogue (eyes go to the bottom left). The Eye movement patterns is a generalization which, of course, does not always apply. Note: For left-handers, the pages are usually interchanged.

Practice:
Watch the eye movements of the participants during interviews on television.

Reframing

“There is nothing that would be good or bad in itself, only thinking makes it so.” – William Shakespeare. You decide what meaning you give to things by placing them in a certain frame. The so-called Reframing something is reversed to achieve a change of perspective.

Reframing is one of the strongest and most commonly used concepts in the NLP. The ability to look at things from a different perspective is the essence of the "Outside the Box" thinking. This is why it is worth understanding and practicing reframing at the level of championships.

Practice:
Welches private Problem kannst Du in eine Herausforderung verwandeln?

Six-Step-Reframing

This format is particularly suitable for behaviors and symptoms whose meaning is not revealed to the consciousness. The 6-step program changes unwanted behavior by making better alternatives available. The core of the intervention is the distinction between intention and behaviour.

  • Step 1: Identify the unwanted behavior
  • Step 2: Communicate with the part of you who is responsible for it
  • Step 3: What is the positive intention behind it?
  • Step 4: Be creative and develop a new behavior
  • Step 5: Introduction of New Behavior for the Future
  • Step 6: Eco-Check

Submodalities

Imagine a positive experience from your life: enlarge this image, add more colour and add sounds and sounds. The Submodalities allow you to quickly act on your own emotional life and get rid of annoying habits or fears.

The development of submodalities:
Person A goes into a positive state. Person B asks the submodalities. After that, Person A goes into a less pleasing state, which he would like to stress later. Person B also asks the submodalities here. The submodalities of the two states can now be compared. What are the differences?

The following questions can be asked:

  • Visual: What do you see? Are you associated or dissociated? Is it a picture or a movie? Rather close or far?
  • Auditive: What do you hear? Are there voices, sounds, sounds or silence? Where are the noises coming from? Sound or quiet?
  • Kinästhetik: What body feelings do you feel? Where in the body do you feel them? Do you feel warm or cold?

Swish technique

Would you like to dissolve an unwanted behavior now? With the Swish technique can change unwanted habits and build strong positive motivation. The submodalities: size, brightness and distance are used in the standard-swish. This works very well in many cases. In short, the target image becomes larger, brighter and comes closer and the unwanted image becomes smaller, darker and removed.

  • Step 1: Find an unwanted behavior
  • Step 2: Imagine the triggering image (associated). What immediately precedes the undesirable behavior?
  • Step 3: Create a target image (dissociated). What can anyone recognize from the outside that you are a person for whom ... is not a problem?
  • Step 4: The Swish. Make a swim with the two pictures. Start by seeing the triggering image of the context big and bright. Then put a small dark picture of the target image into the lower right corner. The small dark image will grow bigger and cover the first image, which will fade and shrink as quickly as you can say “Swish”
  • Step 5: Repeat 7x
  • Step 6: Imagine the first picture. What happens?

Fast-Phobia

With this NLP technique it is possible to eliminate phobias within a short time. Imagine a phobic situation dissociated as a black and white film. Now play the film colour and associated backwards. By applying the phobia after several times dissolved.

  • Step 1: Think about a situation where you responded with stress/ anxiety.
  • Step 2: Now get out of the state.
  • Step 3: Imagine you are in the cinema and would see a black and white film of this situation with a double of you.
  • Step 4: Imagine the inner film and how your double slowly approaches the critical situation and then experiences the stress-inducing event.
  • Step 5: The worst point is to stop the movie, as if you stop a video and a stand image.
  • Step 6: Now go into the film (associated). You are now in the situation and now see the film through your eyes with everything that can be seen in the situation. Now bring color to the picture.
  • Step 7: Let the movie run back quickly until you are safe again. Open your eyes.
  • Step 8: Repeat this format of 3.-7. several times and then test if you are still able to get a stress reaction with the idea of 1. If so, go through the format again. If no, test the result in reality on occasion.

The language is not only the basis for a clear understanding, but also for a quick and targeted change. With the meta-model of language, you can become one of the leading language tools of our time and become a master of the spoken language. The meta model of language is a collection of numerous language patterns and question types. The collection of language patterns can be found here: Meta model.

Practice:
Use the meta model alone by writing a positive or negative experience and then metamodelling yourself: highlighting patterns in the text, questioning and giving each response.

Milton model

The Milton model is a kind of reversal of the meta model. If statements are specified and specified with the meta-model, the Milton model is rather artistic and vague. This unspecific language serves to trigger trance states and help the listener get access to his inner resources. With the help of this Milton model, you can perform a beautiful relaxation trance. However, you can also use the Milton model in business contexts, e.g. in lectures, to put the listeners in beautiful emotional states or in sales talk to create positive associations with their own products.

Time-Line

The Time-Line is a visual symbol, an NLP technique through which you can encode your past, present and future. It serves, for example, to plan targets. How is your timeline? Do you live in time or through time? Use your own timeline to plan your goals systematically and gain clarity for your future.

How do we make it that we encode memories in any way, so that with two different memories we immediately know which is sooner and which later? How does our brain make the difference between the future and the past? The basic hypothesis is that our brain disperses temporal differences. This was empirically confirmed by many people. The discovery of the timeline enables us to carry out a large number of changes in a very short time.

trance

A Trance status is a different state of consciousness than the usual. It is usually rather immobile and focuses attention inward on an internally retrieved or generated experience. The trance state has nothing to do with sleep, but cannot be physiologically demarcated against meditation and relaxation. However, hypnosis not to be equal with relaxation, since trance phenomena can also be generated without physical relaxation and the hypnosis capability cannot be trained in contrast to relaxation. Nevertheless, the trance state is not unique and is not completely different from everyday trances of the state of watch, such as the highway hypnosis or the emergence into a good book.

A method for trance initiation, the 5 4 3 2 1 method:
The method is based on language and is used with pacing and leading. At the beginning, five statements for the immediate experience of the person “gepacet”. Example: “You sit on the chair.”, “You hear my voice.”, “Your hands rest on your thighs.”, “Your eyes are closed.”, “You breathe.” After that, a lead-out message comes with which the one is guided in terms of a goal: “You start to relax more and more.” Then we continue with four pacing statements and two leading statements.

Here is the more overview:

  • Step 1: 5 Pacing Statements + 1 Leading Statement
  • Step 2: 4 Pacing statements + 2 Leading statements
  • Step 3: 3 Pacing statements + 3 Leading statements
  • Step 4: 2 Pacing statements + 4 Leading statements
  • Step 5: 1 Pacing Statement + 5 Leading Statements

Note:
In the 5 4 3 2 1 method always go from outside to inside and from pacing to lead.

SMART method

Maybe you know the following situation. You have a goal in mind, start working and set after some time You realize something doesn't work on it. Objectives should be formulated specifically, measurable, attractive, realistic and scheduled so that they can be achieved more easily and quickly.

  • S(special): A target should be specific, i.e. exactly and accurately formulated.
  • M(possible): A certain measurability should be given so that progress can be seen.
  • A(tractive): The goal should be attractive to you.
  • R(ealistic): The goal should be possible through physical or other laws and personal basic characteristics.
  • T(erminated): Set a time when the target is to be reached.

Practice:
Is there something in your life that you've always wanted to do, but never really did it? If you have found something, then formulate a well-formed goal that is specific, measurable, attractive, realistic and scheduled. Write the target on a sheet of paper and hang it up where you often see it.

Strategies

Strategies are defined as specific awareness plans, with the help of which one organizes his thoughts and behaviour in order to accomplish certain tasks. There are, for example, the Walt-Disney strategy or the TOTE model.

Each strategy also includes certain attitudes and beliefs. For example: “Being successful is possible and important to me.”
Strategies are like the recipe with the help of which we bake a cake: essential are the ingredients, decisive the amount of the respective ingredient (whether an egg or ten) and the order in which we merge them.

Disney Strategy

This macro strategy goes back to Walt Disney, the co-founder of the world-famous Disney Company. Disney was a person with very pronounced dreams and visions. It separated three different phases of the objective and differentiated them both spatially and temporally precisely. These phases or positions were: the creative dreamer, the realistic planner and the constructive critic. For each of these three positions, Walt Disney had another room, which also supported him by his equipment, to get into a mental condition that is favorable for the respective position.

  • Step 1: Mark three different locations in the room.
  • Step 2: In the next steps, the individual positions run relatively quickly to gain a first impression of this strategy.
  • Step 3: Take the position of the dreamer.
  • Step 4: Remember a situation where you were creative and imaginative and develop creative ideas.
  • Step 5: leave the dreamer's position and take a short moment to pass.
  • Step 6: Take the position of the realistic planner. Find out one of your dreamer's ideas that you want to plan now. How could you realize this idea?
  • Step 7: Leave the planner's position.
  • Step 8: Take the position of the constructive critic. Remember a time or a situation where you were constructive and critical, in which you recognized obstacles and objections early and knew exactly where the strengths and weaknesses of a plan lie. Look at the result of the dreamer and the planner. Feel once in and develop a feeling for the plan. Where else is missing? What hasn't been considered yet? Can you achieve your goal with this plan?

Future pace

This is an NLP technique by which the results of a change process can be transferred to the future. The process in which a person explores their future ideas for a specific context, a specific task, etc. Future pace is a standard method of the NLP at the end of modification techniques. A positive future presentation after an NLP intervention is an indication that the desired change can occur naturally and automatically. A negative future presentation is an indication that the NLP intervention used will have little impact on the actual behaviour in the future.

The format uses virtual mentors that allow us to change perspectives and thus expand our own horizon. Through our imagination it tickles out the hidden wisdom in us to get answers.

  • Step 4: Now put yourself in each of your mentors
  • Step 5: Whoever you left the position of the last mentor thinks again

Praliné pattern

A pattern to do something you don't like to do, but you have to do with more motivation. A very sensible application of this pattern is the change of your feelings in tasks to which you have decided congruently, but which you do not like to do at present. If you have decided that it is important to do it, you can enjoy it as well!

  • Step 1: Find a problem
  • Step 2: Motivating image (associated)
  • Step 3: Image of the task (dissociated)
  • Step 4: Eco-Check
  • Step 5: Iris patterns. See the image of the task before your spiritual eye, with the motivating image directly behind it. Quickly open a hole in the front image so you can see the image behind it through this hole. Quickly open the hole as far as you need to achieve a full emotional response to the motivating image. Now let the hole shrink quickly. But only as fast as you can maintain the emotional response to the motivating image
  • Step 6: Repeats
  • Step 7: Test. The goal is to connect the feeling of the motivating image to the task image

Anxiety

Using this technique you can Anxiety make it shrink so that it loses its threatening effect.

  • Step 1: Think of a person in whose presence you feel so far anxious, uncertain, inhibited, nervous and similar. Where and how do you see the picture before your “spiritual eye”? Is it further away or more in front of your nose? Does the person in your imagination have the real size or does it look bigger? What feeling arises in you when you think of this person? ...
  • Step 2: Imagine a small black and white TV, a few meters away from you and left down on the ground.
  • Step 3: See the image of this person now in the small black and white television. What changes in your feeling when you see this person as a small black-and-white image on the bottom left?
  • Step 4: Now let the person's image emerge in the original way before your spiritual eye with his eyes closed. Let the image of the person shrink, and see it as in the small black and white television, left down a few meters from you. Open your eyes.
  • Step 5: Repeat step 4 five to eight times.
  • Step 6: Imagine that you would meet with this person. How do you experience the situation now?
