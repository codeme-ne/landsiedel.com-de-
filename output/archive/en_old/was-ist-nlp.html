What is NLP? NLP simply explained

Reading time: 15 minutes

Neurolinguistic programming, in short NLP, is a model of motivation and communication developed in the 1970s. It has been developed by the analysis of very successful psychotherapists and is now used in a wide range of life areas, such as therapy, sales, leadership, partnership and dealing with children.

In the NLP, discussions, behavioral, hypno- and body-oriented approaches are combined. Our thinking, feeling and behavior (Neuro) is systematically changed (programmed) by means of language (Linguistic). Over the past 25 years, our coach team has conducted nearly 20,000 training days. Our Managing Director is Germany President of the world's largest NLP association.

Where does NLP come from? Origin

Richard Bandler, John Grinder and Frank Pucelik met in the seventies. John Grinder was an assistant professor of linguistics at the University of California in Santa Cruz. Richard Bandler was a mathematician, design therapist and computer expert. Together with Frank Pucelik, they wanted to constantly learn and develop.

They found out how experts achieved very special services in their areas. John Grinder, Richard Bandler and Frank Pucelik started successful people modelling and to identify the patterns they used to achieve their goals. For example, they questioned why some well-known psychotherapists had so much success in treating their clients and others did not. They realized that their work had to be based on a common pattern of behaviour.

Bandler, Grinder and Pucelik developed a model that is applicable to everyone and promotes communication, learning and personal change. They called it "Neurolinguistic programming". Bandler, Grinder and Pucelik gave their knowledge to students in workshops and seminars. They were especially famous for modeling hypnotherapeut Dr. Milton Erickson, family therapist Virgin and shape therapy Fritz Perls.

Is NLP dangerous?

Attention! NLP is dangerous if the user has not completed a professional training that informs about which problems can occur in certain treatment types. In the event of faulty execution, trance inductions, face-therapeutic measures and trauma treatment at client's mental long-term damage.

Many interventions must not be carried out until there is no authorization as a medical practitioner or psychotherapist. Professional NLP trainers know exactly how far their competencies are and where their limits lie in the application.

On the other hand, NLP can be exaggerated Self-worth and omnipotence. Consequently, users may believe that they are invulnerable through knowledge. Completely free from the load of the ability to self-criticism and by self-confirmation, users can lose the ground.

Why NLP is criticised is often due to bias, ignorance, early associations and negative individual experiences of the individual person. Although the roots of the method are anchored in science and there are many studies that speak for their effectiveness, there are still critics who claim NLP is not scientifically based enough.

Others believe in reading thoughts with the help of NLP and manipulate people can. NLP is a danger! An insight into the techniques opens up many new possibilities, which can lead, among other things, to direct the behavior of a person in a preferred direction. Knowledge about itself and the others, and an intensified human knowledge, can be called manipulation.

One should also face the promises of loud marketers with scepticism. "NLP leads in no time Happiness, wealth and success" is a popular phrase that should not be given too much faith. It's not that simple in reality.

What does this have to do with NLP? In NLP, new behavior is made accessible to the brain. The aim of the NLP to no longer deal with a problem, but to put the solution at the centre, also opens up readiness to change. The Associations

NLP Institutes: The International Association of NLP Institutes is the world's largest NLP association with over 10,000 members. For each country, there is its own president, who supervises the master trainer and takes care of the development and compliance of the high standards in terms of quality, professionalism and ethics. The association organizes world congresses and has its own projects that lead NLP in the world. The certificate of this association is included by default.

DVNLP: The German Association for Neuro-Linguistic Programming e.V. is a great expert forum for people who use NLP in a professional and private context. The association sets up curricula and controls compliance with the rules by the instructors. Once a year, a congress and an event called Future Tools will take place.

INLPTA: The International NLP Trainers Association is an international, globally active NLP training company that takes care of quality, professionalism, ethics and business applications in the NLP. It is represented and associated with its own coordinators in China, Japan, USA, Australia, New Zealand and in most European countries.

What does NLP have to do with psychology?

When in the 19th and 20th century psychology almost exclusively concerned with depression and mental illnesses, the American psychologist began Martin to turn around and consciously positive Feelings to take advantage of change. He noted that optimism is learnable and he wanted to promote the strengths of others.

Positive Psychology found that one can increase his well-being and feeling of happiness when using his character strengths. If the person pursues an activity that makes sense for him and uses his strengths, he lives a much healthier life. Happy people live longer and are not so vulnerable to mental illnesses.

Martin Seligman differentiated six virtues with 24 character strengths:

  • Wisdom and knowledge: creativity, curiosity, openness, learning joy, perspective
  • Courage: courage, persistence, integrity, vitality
  • Humanity: love, kindness, social intelligence
  • Justice: social responsibility, fairness, leadership
  • Moderation: forgiveness and compassion, humility, modesty, pity, self-regulation
  • Transcendence: appreciation of beauty and excellence, gratitude, hope, humour, spirituality.

NLP in der Psychotherapie und Beratung

Historically, NLP has its origin in various short-term therapeutic, solution-oriented, hypno-systemic and constructive psychotherapy approaches. In recent years, NLP psychotherapy has been invented – an independent psychotherapy approach, which, however, is struggling for professional recognition. In some practices, psychotherapists offer solution-oriented short-term therapies and use NLP in a targeted manner. Apart from this, there are overlaps in the treatments of classical psychotherapy and neurolinguistic programming. The most famous is the treatment of phobias with NLP. It can help people overcome a phobia within a short time.

NLP offers tools...

  • to establish trusted relationships with different clients,
  • understanding the holistic structure and effect of attitudes, beliefs, values and self-images,
  • to an enhanced understanding of the effect of speech,
  • for process-oriented work, which protects both client integrity and privacy, and facilitates permanent changes,
  • for solution-oriented and short-term therapeutically oriented setting.

NLP, Education and School

The sooner children learn to communicate, the better. Of course, it also depends on what social environment they are currently in and what they are looking at from others. NLP basically promotes communication between parents and children. Adults discover how they can communicate with their children in a positive and understanding way.

By expressing their parents clearly, they enable their child to understand them and react appropriately. The choice of words is particularly important when communicating. Permanent denials may have a negative effect on the behaviour of the child. "Don't tickle" may lead to even more sticking around, as the child, in order to follow this command, must first think about the action.

If you want to understand children, you should put yourself in the world of the child. Only in this way can it be ensured that the message arrives and is understood at all.

  • What does my child already know?
  • How does it feel?
  • Do you understand me?
