VAKOG

Immerse yourself in the sense-specific world of your interlocutor to gain deeper understanding and understanding. This "sensual consciousness" opens to you unfaithful levels of communication.

We experience the world through our sense organs. The external stimuli, e.g. a sunbeam or a friend's voice, are transmitted via our nervous system through electrical or chemical signals to our brain. There, pictures, sounds and other sensations arise. These represent the outer stimulus. That is why we are talking about representation systems in the NLP.

According to the NLP model, people use these five sensory channels with different focus. Some people are highly visually oriented and use little information on the other channels. When people meet with differently dominant representation systems, there can be great misunderstandings.

Examples are:

  • Look how many phrases you can find when you keep your eyes open.
  • Listen to what phrases are coming to your ears!
  • It will be easy for you to combine even more terms.
  • Soon You have a smell for this language and find taste for it!

The preferred or current dominant representation system can be recognized by the prevailing signal words or physical indications, such as, for example, speech speed, breathing, etc. Find out more You have a list of words that indicate a representation system: Three types VAK.

VAKOG kits

Visually oriented people sound like this:

  • Can you show me other houses in this area?
  • I need to see more before I can even imagine making a decision.
  • I already see something clearer, but I want to take a closer look at some of your offers.
  • Look, I'm not sure you showed me everything. I'm still in the dark.

Auditive people sound like this:

  • Tell me more about your product. I've heard a lot of good about it.
  • I'm not satisfied with what you said so far. It's not the first time someone promises me his product would last forever.
  • That sounds very good. Everyone talks about how good your product is.
  • It's always the same thing. You're the best, but I'm trying to tell you that I can't afford your services. I'm going to have to get a little clearer to finally make my voice heard.

Kinesthetically oriented people sound like this:

  • I need your support on this. I don't think I can handle it alone.
  • I don't like to fall into this. I'd rather slow it down, or I'll get out of the rain.
  • I have a good feeling. The matter has regained itself.
  • The presentation impressed me. They really put the cards on the table.

Exercises

The book report

Three people have read the same book and now report on their experiences. Watch the signal words!

  • The first shows that he saw a lot in that the examples were well chosen to illustrate the subject, and that it was written in a shiny style.
  • The second could contradict the sound: it has a shrill prose style. He could not agree with the author's ideas, and he would like to tell him that.
  • The third has the feeling that the book deals in a very balanced way with a weighty theme. He likes the way the author touches all the key topics, and he easily understood the new ideas.

The sales address

  • Dear Lord, as we have already discussed on the phone, here I will send you my book set and a cassette with the last seminar. I hope the language is clear enough for you and I will hear from you again.
  • Dear Lord, I have well looked at the clear words in your letter. I see the problem and send you our photo set and our video tapes. I hope you can watch these videos soon and get a good picture of us. I look forward to your soon reaction. Goodbye!
  • Dear Lord, I was very touched that you visited me recently. Now I can really get into your feelings about your special problem. That's why I'd like to send you my used handkerchief. At the next seminar you can give me your heart, I wish you all the best. With affection and warm greetings.
