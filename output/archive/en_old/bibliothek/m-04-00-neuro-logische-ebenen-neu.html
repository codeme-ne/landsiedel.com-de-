Dilts Pyramid – The neurological levels

Robert Dilts has developed a very wonderful model for the NLP, which we can use very well for our personal development and the description of change processes: the Dilts Pyramide.
Among other things, the model provides valuable suggestions and support for the issues of goals and personality development.

What is the Dilts Pyramid?

The model helps to understand itself and its environment better. At the same time, it should provide an indication of the level at which changes must be made when a personal development is to take place. The logical levels are levels of change. They serve to clarify where, for example, a problem, a goal or a mission is settled. The logical levels can be used, for example, in problem solving and resource work.

Instructions and questions make the resources aware in each level. Those are then introduced into the underlying. However, a frequent misunderstanding of this valuable concept arises from the name “logical planes”, because they are not really logical (as logic units define this term) or are precisely defined levels. For this reason, the Dilts Pyramid is spoken today.

  • Pyramid of motivation
  • Model for structuring change processes
  • Higher levels influence deeper levels i.d.R. more than vice versa
  • The solution of a problem is rarely found at the level of the problem
  • One of my most important coaching models

Robert Dilts

Robert B. Dilts geboren in den USA, ist ein Autor, Trainer, Berater und Entwickler in der Disziplin des Neuro-Linguistic Programming (NLP). He pioneered the introduction of the NLP's applications into the areas of organisational development, leadership and learning. In more than 30 countries he works with companies such as: The World Bank, Isvor Fiat, IBM Europe.

He designed the model of logical levels in the mid-1980s. He has formulated them in accordance with Bateson's logical levels of learning. In the NLP, they have gained immense importance since their publication. They are probably released for the first time in 1990 in “Changing Beliefs with NLP”.

The Dilts Pyramid Layers

At the lowest level, theEnvironment“ (1st level) man moves with his “Behavior‘ (2nd level).

There is no doubt that theSkills“ (3rd level) that make it possible to perform any behavior at all.

Behind/about the skills is the motivation to want to do a behavior, or the ability to develop and practice. Since this motivation is not only driven, we are not only talking about motives at this point, but generally about "Values and beliefs“ (4th level). These constructs (model of the world) we use as criteria to decide what we believe, i.e. to think right, true and important. In the NLP, we also like to talk to Neudeutsch about “Beliefs”, because in English the separation between “belief” (belief) and “religious believe” (faith) is clearer than in German.

In the 5th level we define our "Identification‘ (5th level). We thus distinguish who we are, what we are, what belongs to us and what is not.

In the 1990s, Dilts added another level above the identity. Transcendently, he speaks of both “cirity” and “spirituality”. In the course of the further development of the NLP, he laid a foundation for what he calls “third generation NLP” in recent years. Often it is for this 6. Level also the term ‘Vision“used. Since “spirituality” can be quickly misunderstood, I personally like to use the concepts of vision and belonging for this 6th and most important level in my world.

Environment

At the bottom level is the surrounding area. Each event takes place in a temporal and spatial context. The level of the environment contains all external conditions that affect a person. They can be experienced externally with the senses and can be experienced with the questions “Where?”, “Wann?”, “Who?”, “Who?”, “Who else?”.

Behavior

The level of behavior refers to all externally perceptible actions and reactions of a person: their actions, their words, their choice of votes, their gestures, their movements, their breathing. The behavior can be described with sinne-specific beads. This is about what?

Skills

Skills are cognitive and emotional processes that a person passes through so that a certain behavior becomes possible. They are not perceptible from the outside. This is about skill, thinking and feeling, also about consciousness about these skills. The question about this is “How?”.

Values and beliefs

The next level are the Principles, the beliefs, the values, the inner criteria that are based on action (conscious and unconscious), are the actual motives of a person, guiding ideas that they consider true and used to use their skills. People use existing skills only if there are appropriate beliefs and criteria that allow this. Faith sets are interpretations from previous experiences. They are individual theories why something is so and not different.

Identification

The next level is the level of identity: the self-image, the ideas that a person constructs about himself and his behavior, his skills and beliefs mostly unconsciously. Identity – with the poles of personal and social identity – can also be considered as a special ammunition of faith. It is the central model of the overall personality.

Vision

The highest level is the level of belonging or mission, vision. This is about our professional, family, social or perhaps philosophical, religious affiliation and vision. This spiritual level guides and forms our life and gives our existence a basis. This is about the “big questions” in life: “Why do we live?”, “Why are we here?” “What is the meaning of life?”

The Dilts Pyramid using an example

In connection with stress, we want to use the neurological levels according to Dilts to identify stressors and activate resources that help us to counteract stress. There are numerous NLP intervention techniques at each level, which help to bring about the desired changes.

Environment

  • What in the area triggers stress?
  • Answers could be: loud office, dispute with colleagues, meeting in the morning etc.

To reduce stress, it helps, for example, not to carry out certain work in a loud environment or to shift it to another time of day in which there is still silence in the office. At this level, you are looking for lack of resources to help avoid stress within the individual environment.

Behavior

  • What does my behaviour contribute to stress?
  • Answers could be: I constantly change my daily routines take dates last second true etc.

After answering this question, a look will be made for useful behaviors that can serve as a resource. For example, one would be that you take time to relax during the lunch break, or you can relax after work. In order to change behavior, the NLP has a comprehensive collection of tools. Stress can be effectively reduced by a targeted influencing of one's physiology (e.g. the change in one's own posture, breathing, voice).

In dealing with severe stressors, the VK dissociation thus formed has proven itself very well. This technique allows you to see how you encounter the stressor on a cinema screen. With targeted influence, it is now possible to look at the situation calmly and calmly and thus prepare the way for a change of behaviour. Methods of classic conditioning – in the NLP “Anchor‘and Relaxation methods are used here.

Skills

  • How to perform the activities, which internal processes and programs run?
  • Answers could be: I can't concentrate. I can't be on time because there's always something in between.

In order to activate resources at this point, it is helpful if the client learns to imagine how he can develop the missing skills, for example to appear on time at his appointments, and then to ask what he has done to achieve the positive goal.

At this level, strategies play a special role in the NLP. Those describe the internal processes that lead to a certain result. It is important to interrupt inappropriate stress strategies and learn new, effective alternatives. These are derived from already successful models. Thus, in a relatively short time, one can develop very effective new skills in dealing with one's own stress factors.

Values and beliefs

  • What beliefs could cause stress?
  • Answers could be: I must be able to read the wishes of others from their lips. I always have to make everything perfect.

“All beliefs that make you dependent on the grace of other people or on certain events, or which do not leave any alternatives to action, contribute to the emergence of stress,” said Ian McDermott and Joseph O'Connor, who, in their book “NLP and Health”, have dedicated their own, very detailed chapter to stress. They suggest that all beliefs that strengthen the sense of influence are resources.

Identification

  • Who am I (if I live these beliefs, abilities)?
  • Answers could be: I'm a slave of my schedule. I'm a servant for the others.

Healthy self-confidence is a good source of energy to prevent stress. Self-confident people know who they are and therefore what they can do for themselves. Stress arises at this level when your own personality is not lived. This can be expressed in that it is hidden and no feelings are shown or by wearing a mask that protects from others but also from itself.

Here it is important to penetrate deeper into true personality. For example, one can ask oneself very consciously: “Who am I really?” and “How am I?”. From the demarcation, you can learn a lot about yourself: “How am I not?”, “How do I not want to be?”. There are NLP Methodsto explore his own identity. In doing so, one becomes aware of its characteristics (e.g. passionate, patient, vulnerable, etc.), which are checked below: Where are there reference experiences that “reveal” that someone has these qualities? What's someone doing? Are there any experiences in which the opposite has been voted? These experiences are questioned. The self-image becomes clearer and clearer in pieces.

Vision

  • Is there on the professional, private, ideal, philosophical level something or someone or group you know you belong to? What task do I have in this world?
  • Behind the answers to these questions are very great resources.

Those who find good answers for themselves and can live these will no longer feel their stress as such. He will be glad and grateful to live a little more his destiny every day.

The boundaries of the current identity can be broken with this consciousness and integrated into a comprehensive sense of self. This can be achieved through different ways, such as meditation, spiritual experiences, love, etc.

Application

  1. Part: Leading the target state through the neurologic levels (NE)


    • First a short lead with A Objective according to the known criteria (positive, self-reachable, contextualized, mind-specific, ecological).
    • Join the "as-ob-frame" for the entire exercise now: "Assume you have reached your goal now..." and lead A in this state through the entire NE planes. Start with the level environment and proceed step by step from there and anchor the situation with a space anchor and perhaps with terms or symbols.

      If at one or more levels there is evidence that resources are still lacking, then go first to the next higher level and find here possibly the resources that were still lacking at the previous stage. Otherwise, just notice the level where something is missing for later.

    Coaching questions on the different levels:

    Environment: Where are you here? Who are you with? What do you hear? What do you see? What do you taste? What do you smell?

    Behavior: What are you doing here?

    Skills: How do you do what you do? How do you relate to others? What special skills do you have here?

    Faith and Values : What is important to you here? Why are you doing what you do? What do you think about? What motivates you? What do you think about yourself, others, your job, etc?

    Identification: What kind of self-understanding do you have here? How do you understand yourself, who are you?

    Membership: Where are you? Is there something or someone or a group on the professional, private, ideal, spiritual levels that you know are part of? What task do you have?

    Go a step further.
    Your unconscious will give you another important information at this point. It can be an idea, an image, a symbol, a feeling or whatever. It is a special gift from your unconscious to you, for your goal. Take as much time as you need to take this all in you.

  2. Part: Collect resources


    • Now go back and remember all the insights and resources you have already found on this path. Experience how each level has enriched and intensified through the knowledge and experience of others. Take the changes.
    • If something is missing at any stage, go to dissociation and give resources from here (send them or set a resource anchor). Go back to the relevant level and experience the change that has now arisen with the resources.
    • Continue to the level of environment and remember your goal. Experience how your goal has become richer compared to the beginning.

  3. Part: Internalization and completion


    • Now run all levels once again at your own pace. Take the time you need.
    • Now pass through the entire levels from top to bottom without thinking in a quick pass. Come back to the starting position and see how your goal has become richer.
    • Future Pace: When will you take the first steps to complete your goal?
