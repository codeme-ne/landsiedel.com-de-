Bert Hellinger - Developer of the Family Performance

Pioneer of systemic therapy and family tasks

Bert Hellinger (born Anton Hellinger, 16. December 1925 – †19. September 2019) was a German psychotherapist, theologian and author. He was known internationally as the founder of the Family tasks (also called system setup), a method of systemic therapy. His work strongly shaped the psychotherapeutic landscape and at the same time triggered considerable controversies – both due to his therapeutic practice and his later spiritual views.

Early years and embossing

Bert Hellinger was born on 16 December 1925 in Leimen (Baden) under the name Anton Hellinger. He grew up in Cologne in a strictly Catholic family. His father was a railway, strictly religious and authoritarian in his educational style, which burdened the relationship between father and son. The political situation in Nazi Germany left traces: as a member of a forbidden Catholic youth movement, he was observing the Gestapo.

After World War II, he joined the Order of Mariannhill missionaries, studied philosophy and Catholic theology and was consecrated to the Catholic priest. As a member of the Order, Hellinger named Suitbert, abbreviated as "Bert". In South Africa, Hellinger worked with Zulu communities for 16 years. This time deeply shaped him: Zulu culture, dealing with conflicts, spirituality and healing left permanent impressions that should later be reflected in his therapeutic work.

Training for therapists

After returning to Europe in the 1960s, he left the Order and placed the priesthood. He completed a psychoanalytical training in Vienna and began intensively dealing with various forms of psychotherapy:

  • Formal therapy (to Fritz Perls)
  • Primary therapy (to Arthur Janov)
  • Transaction analysis (after Eric Berne)
  • Hypnotherapy (to Milton Erickson)
  • Systemic family therapy (to Virginia Satir)

From the synthesis of these influences, especially the family reconstruction of Virginia Satir, he developed his own method: Family job.

The development of family work

In the 1980s/1990s Hellinger started with what was later called a classic family job. The "Deputy representative" Family members symbolically set up in space to make unconscious entanglements visible within a family system. Hellinger observed that people often took fates of previous generations – such as guilt, loss or trauma – to secure their belonging. He called this Systemic knitting.

How does a family job work?

In the case of the family job after Hellinger, any person from the circle of the attendees will be arranged spatially so as to correspond to his perception of the family situation. The change in the view of the problems of the client is then due to intellectual and emotional insights from a "knowing field" to carry out.

Hellinger later developed the so-called "Movies of the Soul and the Spirit", in which the deputies leave their motion impulses silently from the role and the systemic field. This usually leads to dramatic, mostly loose motion images.

International Effect & Developments

Bert Hellinger was known worldwide. Its method has been taken up in many countries, particularly in Germany, Austria, Switzerland, Russia, Latin America and Asia, and has sustainably influenced systemic therapy and family therapy. Numerous schools and therapists further developed his work:

  • Organisational tasks (Systemanalyse in Unternehmen)
  • Structure tasks (after Matthias Varga von Kibéd and Insa Sparrer)
  • symptom orders (psychosomatic complaints)
  • Dream tasks (in combination with trauma therapy)

Building on his legacy, the German Society for Systemic Therapy (dgsf) was founded, which aims to establish standards for training, quality assurance and ethics in the field of system tasks.

Controversy & criticism

Hellinger was controversial in specialist circles. Critics accused him, among others:

  • an authoritarian occurrence in seminars and demonstrations
  • a missing scientific finding of his method
  • a simplified representation of complex psychic processes
  • Border crossings in installation processes
  • esoteric-spiritual world images that were perceived as dogmatic

Despite the criticism, his work remained a valuable stimulus for many – especially in the context of personal and transgenerational healing processes.

Quotes from Bert Hellinger

"What is recognized can go. What is displaced remains."

Important publications

  • "Ordnungen der Liebe" (1994) – Basic work on his concepts
  • "Centre and Periphery" – Continued Hellinger’s Method
  • "Return to love" – About binding, partnership, solution
  • "The source does not need to ask where it flows" – spiritual deepenings
  • "Recognise what works" – Interviews about his life work

Personal life and death

Bert Hellinger was married twice. He met his first wife after leaving the Order, with her he had three children. In the 1990s he married Sophie Hellinger in second marriage. Together they founded the international institute HellingerIleben®.

In the late years of his life, he retired from public work, but continued to be a writer and a spiritual teacher. He died 19. September 2019 im Alter von 93 Jahren in Bad Reichenhall.

Bert Hellinger's legacy continues to live in systemic therapy and family work and is still inspired by therapists and coaches worldwide.
