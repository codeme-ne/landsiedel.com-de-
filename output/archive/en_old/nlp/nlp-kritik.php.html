What is NLP anyway?

Neuro-Linguistic programming ‚Äì short NLP ‚Äì sounds at first glance something very technical. In fact, however, it is a psychological approach to help you improve communication, thinking and behavior. NLP has been widely used in coaching, personal development and sales for decades.

Origin and destination of NLP

Das Konzept wurde in den 1970er Jahren von Richard Bandler and John Grinder developed. Their goal was to analyze successful communication patterns from therapists such as Milton Erickson or Virginia Satir ‚Äì and to transfer them to other people.

NLP is based on the assumption that our thinking (Neuro), our language (linguistics) and our behavior (programming) are connected to each other. So if you recognize and change your inner patterns, you should also be able to control your behavior and improve your results.

What is NLP used for?

Many use NLP techniques in their everyday life without even knowing. Typical applications are:

  • Improving communication ‚Äì for example in relationships or in a professional context
  • Achieving objectives faster ‚Äì through clear target definitions and mental visualization
  • Dissolving blockages ‚Äì for instance in fears, negative beliefs or self-doubts
  • Sales and conviction ‚Äì especially popular in marketing or coaching

This is not only about classical conversation techniques, but also about body language, inner images, belief systems and routines.

NLP: A tool box for changes

NLP does not understand itself as a closed form of therapy, but rather as practical tool box. You take what works for you. Whether this is a specific question technique, an anchor for motivation or a method for stress regulation ‚Äì it is always about direct application in everyday life.

Important is: NLP does not promise miracles, but it can ‚Äì properly applied ‚Äì be an effective tool to advance your personal development.

Why is NLP criticized?

So popular Neuro-Linguistic Programming (NLP) is also ‚Äì it does not remain without controversy. Many experts express legitimate doubts about the effectiveness and seriousness of the approach. When you deal with NLP, you should not only be the chances, but also the Critical aspects know.

scientifically controversial

One of the most common allegations: NLP is not scientifically proven. Many of the techniques are based on subjective observations instead of solid empirical research. Meta-analysis also shows that there are hardly any stressable studies that show NLP a reliable effect.

Especially in the psychological and therapeutic field, NLP does not provide a clearly defined methodology and hardly uses comprehensible mechanisms. The approach often acts as a ‚Äúconstruction box‚Äù of various psychological ideas ‚Äì without a uniform foundation.

Overriding promises

Another criticism concerns the way NLP is marketed. Many providers lure with statements like:

  • ‚ÄúWith NLP you become more successful, happier and richer.‚Äù
  • ‚ÄúResolve your fears in a few minutes.‚Äù
  • ‚ÄúMove your life with just one session.‚Äù

Such Promise of salvation raise false expectations ‚Äì especially in people in crisis situations. Criticists therefore accused NLP-Coaches, emotional dependence to create or use people who hope for quick solutions.

Since there are no uniform standards for training in the NLP, in principle each person can be referred to as ‚ÄúNLP-Coach‚Äù or ‚ÄúMaster Practitioner‚Äù. The result: great quality differences ‚Äì and sometimes very high prices for seminars that convey more marketing than real competence.

Does NLP still work?

Despite the many criticism remains Neuro-Linguistic Programming (NLP) for many people an effective tool. In practice, many users report positive changes ‚Äì even if the scientific evidence is rather thin. So: Does NLP still work?

The answer is not quite simple. But she's somewhere between Placebo effect, subjective experience and targeted observation of self.

Counting personal experiences

Many people experience NLP as helpful ‚Äì be it in coaching, overcoming internal blockades or reaching personal goals. The following aspects seem to work well in practice:

  • Better self-perception ‚Äì through accurate monitoring of language and behaviour
  • Increase motivation ‚Äì using methods such as anchoring or target visualization
  • Improving communication ‚Äì mainly through conscious use of language patterns

NLP can be an entry for you to deal with your thoughts, feelings and patterns. It is not a miracle remedy ‚Äì but a tool that can help you live more consciously. Those who deal with it openly, but also critically, can benefit.

Conclusion: Is NLP good or bad?

As with many methods in the field of coaching and personality development, the answer is also NLP (Neuro-Linguistic programming): It depends.

NLP is not a miracle remedy ‚Äì but also not per se dangerous or useless. is decisive, how you deal with, which expectations you have and from whom you will accompany you.

NLP has potential ‚Äì but with caution

On the one hand, NLP can help you improve your communication, understand yourself better and work specifically on you. Many people experience significant changes, especially in the professional context or in personal growth.

On the other hand, it lacks scientific evidence. There are also black sheep in the industry that advertise with excessive promises or push people into expensive programs. Therefore, when selecting an NLP offer, you should pay attention to the following points:

Is NLP scientifically recognized?

No. NLP is largely rejected by scientific psychology, as there are no sufficient empirical studies showing the effectiveness of its methods. Many techniques are known, but often not measurable or not unambiguously attribute NLP.

Can NLP be dangerous?

Basically no ‚Äì but it depends on the context. If NLP is used by poorly trained coaches or in incorrect relationships (e.g. in mental illnesses), it may be problematic. As a self-experience tool, it is usually harmless as long as you have realistic expectations.

What do I recognize reputable NLP providers?

Pay attention to the following points:

  • No exaggerated promises
  • Transparent content & clear method description
  • Long-standing practical experience instead of ‚Äúcertificates‚Äù
  • Clear distinction for therapy

A good provider will not promise you the blue from heaven ‚Äì but honestly tell you what NLP can and not.

Is NLP the right thing for me?

That depends on what you're looking for. If you want to work on your communication, formulate goals more clearly or solve blockages, NLP can give you valuable impulses. However, if you want to edit deeper psychic topics, you should better use professional help.
