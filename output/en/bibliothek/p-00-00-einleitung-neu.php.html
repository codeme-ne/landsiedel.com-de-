NLP Introduction and Overview

NLP is a modern communication tool and an instrument for changing your own personality. It can be used both in the therapeutic field and in business and many other contexts. Here you will get an overview of the NLP content, basic assumptions, founders, history, learning stages and definitions of NLP.

What does NLP mean?

NLP stands for neurolinguistic programming. Here the three components of the name are explained:

Neuro, Linguistic, Programming.

Neuroscience

Stands for our nervous system. Each behavior is controlled by neuronal processes. Our perception is about our five senses. The information is transmitted through our nervous system in our body. Our nervous system includes our brain and spinal cord. From there, our thinking, feeling and behavior is controlled.

Linguistic

We communicate with other people about our language. At the same time, communication takes place within ourselves. So we have an external and internal communication.

Programming

Our learned behaviour and thinking can be changed through targeted intervention. We can program ourselves to our desired behavior. Changes do not have to be left to chance, but can take place systematically.

Definitions of NLP

Here you will find classic definitions as well as simplified definitions that give you a first idea what NLP is.

NLP stands for neurolinguistic programming and is a communication and motivation model developed in the USA in the 1980s. It was developed by the linguistic professor John Grinder and the mathematician and design therapist Richard Bandler through the modelling of outstanding therapists and can be used in his current form in almost any living area - leadership, therapy, sports, health, sales, rhetoric, relationship.

NLP offers a variety of methods to make its feelings as well as behavioral and thought patterns aware and to develop them in a targeted and constructive manner. In addition, NLP also offers many opportunities to discover and develop personal strengths and skills.

NLP understands itself as a method by which one can learn to become aware of one's own and strange perceptions, expressions and automatic thinking and behavior patterns and to expand or change them (with the help of the inner, partially unconscious potential). Behind the concept is a long-term research on the structure of subjective experiences (how people perceive and react to the world), of communication (how people express and communicate linguistically) and behaviour patterns (how people store and automatically repeat). Particular attention is given to: Strategies successful people who are exactly described and thus made accessible and learnable to anyone who wants this. The findings and content of the NLP contribute to the improvement of communication between people and provide a variety of techniques for targeting and solving problems.

"The NLP-Practitioner-Kartei" by Trageser and von Münchhausen, 2003.

NLP is the art and science of personal perfection, of efficient communication and excellence. "art" because everyone brings his unique personality and style into what he does, and this can never be captured in words or techniques. "science" because there is a method and a method to discover the patterns used by outstanding individuals in all only possible areas to achieve excellent results. This method is called modeling, modeling, and modeling, and the patterns, skills and techniques that have been discovered are increasingly being used in consulting, education and education and in the business world for more effective communication, personal growth and accelerated learning. NLP is a practical skill that brings us the results that we really want to achieve in this world, while at the same time we create something valuable for others. It is exploring what makes the difference between the outstanding and the average.

NLP History

On this page you will find a brief description of the history of the NLP, who were the people involved and how NLP developed. You can get a detailed presentation on the NLP history page.

The history of the NLP begins in the seventies of the twentieth century when the two American scientists Richard Bandler and John Grinder met. John Grinder was an assistant professor of linguistics at the University of California in Santa Cruz, USA. The two decided to find out how experts achieve very special services in certain areas while others fail. These successful people took them as models and analyzed the patterns that these people use to achieve their desired goals.

Grinders and bandlers have become famous primarily through the Modeling of four very fascinating personalities: Dr. Milton Erickson, probably the most important hypnotherapists in the world, Virginia Satir, an exceptional family therapist, Fritz Perls, the father of form therapy and Gregory Bateson, an incredibly intellectual and versatile philosopher and anthropologist.

Nachdem sie die Muster dieser Meister der Kommunikation erkannt hatten, begannen sie diese in Workshops und Seminaren an Studenten weiterzugeben und weiterzuentwickeln.

In the meantime, NLP has spread throughout the world and is used in numerous areas such as therapy, relationship, sale, sports and education.

Model of learning stages

Das Modell der Lernstadien beschreibt, in welchen Entwicklungsschritten sich unser Lernen vollzieht.


Unconscious incompetence
One has never thought about anything before, and therefore it has not been noticed that one cannot.		e.g. a person drives in the car, but the question of his own driving licence has not yet emerged.
Conscious incompetence
Knowledge that one cannot (so must learn and practice if one wants to).		for example, you would like to make the driver's license and sit on the wheel for the first time in a car.
Conscious competence
Conscious appropriation and application of newly learned skills.		e.g., you need to concentrate fully while driving and pay attention to everything that is to be done.
Unconscious competence
Automatic and unconscious application of new skills.		e.g. car driving is so natural that you can talk at the same time.

Advance of the NLP

In the NLP some useful precautions were formulated. These are not objective facts, but they facilitate communication with others and their own lives. Here we go to Advance of the NLP.

Work with beliefs

Success blockades are only too often in ourselves. Sometimes they are completely unconscious to us, but often they appear in the form of sentences that go through our head when we start a project or get an order. They are Principles as: "I am too young/old. I don't have money. I don't have enough training/experience. I don't have good relationships. I come from simple conditions. I don't deserve it. I'm not worth it" etc. Our beliefs form the backbone of our realities. Learn what influence these sentences have on your life, how they have emerged and how you can change them with you and others. Train to create more useful beliefs. You can use a variety of techniques and methods, including Sleight-of-Mouth-Pattern, Dickens Pattern, New Behavior Generator and many more.

Work with values

Values like freedom, love, honesty or success are our inner motivations. They ultimately determine and steer our actions. Because of them, wars are waged, miserable torment and people are destroyed. Nevertheless, most people are not even aware of their own values and much less are the values of their partners, friends and customers. We often get into conflict when we do not have a clear order in our values and demand our highest values to opposite actions, e.g. in the question of career or family. In this block, you learn how values arise and how to find out your personal value hierarchy. You deal with your value conflicts and living rules and train in a conversation to find out the values and living rules of your partner or colleague. If you trade in harmony with your values, you will feel a new, strong energy in you.

Advanced hypnosis techniques

Advanced methods of clinical hypnosis are trained: trance inductions and trance phenomena, posthypnotic orders, ideomotoric signals, self-hypnosis, advanced methods of development of metaphors and the use of trance elements in everyday communication processes.

Meta programs

Meta programs are person-specific perception filters that become visible in the behavior of a person. They process, form and design that information from the outside world that we allow to get inside. Learn to recognize and use these programs with you and others.

Advanced life design

With the help of advanced time-line methods, you will further design your life, edit trauma and shape your future.

Modeling

Learn how to replicate human excellence at an advanced level. With this block, the circle of NLP beginnings closes to today's applications. You learn to find out the patterns of successful people and to make them usable for yourself and others.
