What are strategies?

Strategies are the way we organize our thoughts and behavior to fulfill a task.

There are so-called macro and micro strategies. If, for example, someone has the task of becoming a successful sociologist, the Macroeconomics the step-by-step development of this career: study, doctorate with summa cum laude. Publications, employment at a prestigious university, habilitation, etc.

The Micro strategies e.g. the way in which the person concerned successfully and efficiently learns, writes or presents himself. These microstrategies can be analyzed as certain processes within the sensor systems. They describe a specific internal way of processing sense perceptions, i.e. strategies are formal structures - specific procedures - which are initially completely independent of the respective content.

Each strategy also includes certain settings and Principles. In this case, for example, it is possible and important for me to succeed. I am talented and have the skills to such a career, etc.

Strategies are like Recipient, with the help of which we bake a cake: essential are the ingredients, decisive the amount of the respective ingredient (whether an egg or ten) and the order in which we merge them. It makes a difference whether we add the egg before, during or after baking in the oven. That is, the order of what we do within a strategy is just as important as what we do, even if everything happens within a few seconds. The ingredients of a strategy are the Representation systems, and the quantities and qualities are the Submodalities. The recipe is the great line of the strategy, the individual steps such as stirring the dough are the microunits of this strategy. The beliefs during successful baking would be: baking a cake is possible/I can learn and do it successfully, the cake will taste.

In the NLP we investigate such strategies with the aim of finding out what someone does exactly if he does something successfully to make this ability accessible to others who have not yet been available. In other words, strategies are an essential part of the Modeling.

We also analyze strategies that are less successful to find out how they can be improved.

There are different types of strategies, namely decision-making, motivation, learning, creativity, Relaxation and growth strategies, flirting strategy, partner selection strategies to name a few.

The term strategies in the NLP designate mental processes that allow a person to translate their skills into concrete behaviors in accordance with their beliefs and values. In the NLP, we assume that these processes are conscious and/or unconscious by a sequence of sensory representations (VAKOG) can be described. These VAKOGs, which lead to a certain action, are generally in part purely internal processes, partly directed outwards.

Conditions of structural welfare of strategies

A. A strategy must have a sensory definite goal.

A strategy should have an operation for information collection and feedback, with which a representation of the desired target can be established and/or modified.

B. A strategy must contain all three main representation systems (V, A, K).

Each representation system (RS) has skills to record and process information that the others do not have.

C. A strategy cannot reach the starting point without going through the decision point.

  1. No strategy without EXIT point: A loop (Loop) can occur if the operating phase is so minimal that a significant change can occur by testing representations (comparison/contrast). However, this can also be done by an inadequate test.
  2. No two-point loop: Such a loop is created if a person only swings back and forth between two RS.
  3. Possibilities to stop a strategy with continuous loop: count or time! After the loop has made a certain number of loops or the strategy must come to the decision point.

D. A strategy should have an external check after n steps

n depends on the type of activity. This check is necessary to get the external information you need when drawing, operating, playing piano or the like.

Installing a Strategy

Installing a strategy means making a strategy (which it does not yet have in its repertoire) available to a person so that this strategy can proceed "automatically" in the future. Possibilities are:


  1. the use of a chain anchor (where the individual links of the chain correspond to individual steps of the new strategy),
  2. the conscious insertion into the strategy sequence. Here, for example, a person is encouraged to change their body in accordance with the access instructions of the new strategy (e.g. to make the right movements with the eyes),
  3. interrupting existing, strongly grounded strategies, e.g.

    • (a) By overloading with (to) many (new) sensual impressions, or
    • b) by distraction (conflict)

Redefinition of anchor

In essence, Anchor any representation (internal or external) that triggers a different representation, a quadruple or a series of representations or quadruples (i.e. a strategy). Any part of an experience can be used as an anchor to trigger another part of the experience

Anchors and access signals are the two most important tools in utilising strategies. We can systematically initiate appropriate representations in the right place within a strategy. Access signals can be imagined as self-established anchor.

Planning of context markings and decision points

Old results and related strategies that have become inappropriate for a large part of situations can continue to be effective in a few contexts. In such cases, the programmer installs a decision point where any representation serves as a context marker. This is intended to indicate in which situations what strategy is appropriate. If this measure is omitted, then the ambiguity or overlap of the two contexts can cause a disturbance, which could trigger both strategies at the same time. The person does not know what strategy to apply. It is immobilized by its response to both strategies. Something you say to do, but something else looks more appropriate or feels better.

The representation that serves as a marking can accept any content. It could be a certain sound threshold, a certain word or a certain class of words, a positive or negative kinesthetic feeling or an image or a distinction that is perceived in the environment. The purpose of the signal is to distinguish which context is suitable for which strategy.

  2. The client Strategy sequence let in (form of self-anchor).

Always use both: While the person is "guided" by the strategy, fire the anchor you set up before.

  2. Die Strategiesequenz muss mit dem passenden Kontext verbunden sein - die Sequenz ist an einen Stimulus (an eine Kontextmarkierung) gebunden bzw. geankert.

Installation with the help of anchors

There is a basic difference between Anchors within the framework of Utilisation prozeduren und dem Ankern im Rahmen der Installation. Bei der Utilisation ankerst Du, um den Inhalt bestimmter Strategieschritte zu kontrollieren. Beim Installieren musst Du den Strategieschritt selbst kontrollieren. In diesem Falle wirst Du also nicht einen bestimmten Inhalt, sondern den Akt der Benutzung eines bestimmten Representation system kernel for a specific step. You establish the anchors so that this access to the use of certain representation systems or a sequence of representation systems is created.

A. Anchoring an entire strategy sequence

When Strategy sequence is already present, then it can be anchored with a single anchor so that it can be inserted as a whole in a new sequence which is just designed.

It can also be inserted into a situation where it was not present as a resource beforehand; it is then bound to contextual stimuli that characterize this situation and thus allows a different behavior within this context. It is installed as a resource in situations where the client wishes choices in its objective.

In such cases, the strategy is usually taken out of a context in which it naturally occurs, and installed in a context in which it does not occur or has not yet occurred. In this operation, it is important that you separate the strategy sequence as such from the "trigger" (from the triggering stimulus) that previously triggered this sequence in the context from which you take it out.

Step 1 shows that a specific external, visual Stimulus in context A initiates the strategy sequence Aid .... Ke naturally. This entire sequence is now anchored with the anchor. In step 2, the strategy unit is anchored in the context B in which a specific external one has hitherto been auditorium Stimulus had initiated a Ki - Vi loop. The person is now accessible as a resource from the context A in context B.

Breaking strategies

Sometimes an existing strategy runs on evolved paths or is unusually heavily grounded. If the result of this old strategy interferes with your attempts and if it causes behaviors that do not allow metaresults to be achieved, then it is best for the client to interrupt the existing strategy.

There are three basic ways of interrupting a strategy:


  1. by overload
  2. by deflection and
  3. by "Extruding"The strategy

  • 1) Overload occurs when more information flows into a strategy or strategy step than it can process. "Absolutely feelings, stunned by smell, etc."

    The behavioral result of the overload is that a person's strategy cannot complete its cycle. If a strategy is completely interrupted, the individual will stand without a next step of behavior and will start to the next step of behavior offered to him out of the situation. People in such a situation have the strong tendency to each Anchor to react for reactions that are offered to them from the situation. You can use this phenomenon to install a new strategy if you tune your anchors correctly.
  • 2) A strategy is deflected when a specific input moves the representation sequence away from the current strategy. A person who is sunk in thought is interrupted when a noise or a movement directs his attention to the outer environment. The stimulus does not overload the person's strategy, rather it goes beyond the current sequence and shows the behavior to another place.

    Strategies can be interrupted and distracted if you move your hands back and forth in front of a person's eyes and thereby their Headings disturbed. A depressant can be quickly and effectively extracted from its negative state when you ask him to sit down, keep your head up, get full in the chest.
  • 3) Interruption by "spinning" of a strategy

    A strategy "spins out" when the end of the strategy is anchored with its beginning, so that the strategy is reconnected again and again (like the snake in the proverb that closes its tail). Because the strategy cannot come to an end, it is forced to continue in a loop. In most strategies, a test is built-in - one could call it a meta-test, which can convert the program into a completely new strategy if the strategy operations prove to be ineffective after a certain time - for this purpose the "spinning" exists.
