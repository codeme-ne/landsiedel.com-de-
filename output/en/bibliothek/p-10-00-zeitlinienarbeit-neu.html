Timeline work

Use your timeline to make a systematic planning of your goals and gain clarity in the integration of your personal experiences in the past for your future. Develop an irresistible vision.

Elitising the timeline

On this page, the basic idea of the timeline is described and how to discover the timeline to use you for later work.

Basic idea:
How do we make it that we encode memories in any way, so that we immediately know with two different memories, which is earlier and which is later? How does our brain make the difference between the future and the past?

The basic hypothesis is that our brain disperses temporal differences. This was empirically confirmed by many people.

The discovery of the timeline enables us to carry out a large number of changes in a very short time.

Basic idea:
Take an everyday action like cooking coffee, brushing teeth, dressing, showering or similar.

1. Past

Remember (or just pretend you could) when you

  • have cooked coffee this morning
  • cooked coffee a week ago
  • a year ago...
  • five years ago...
  • 10 years ago...

Do as if now You can all perceive these memories at the same time: what do you notice that a memory is older than the other?

Useful questions

Are these memories, for example, all in the same place or are they rather at different places or at different distances? Are they all in the same brightness/color etc?

Is there anything to hear and if yes, is it equal to loud/leise, the same sound quality etc?

Do you have the same feeling/a different/no one?


Two. Future

Imagine (or just pretend you could) as you

  • tomorrow to cook coffee
  • in einer Woche Kaffee kochen wirst
  • in einem Jahr ....
  • in five years...
  • in zehn Jahren ......

Do as if now You perceive all this at the same time: what do you think a situation is more in the future than another?

3. The entire timeline

Do as if you could now perceive these situations from the future and past at once:
What do you think a situation has passed and the other one is in the future?
Was ist der entscheidende Unterschied in den Submodalities (VAKOG) of past and future?

4. Paint your timeline!


Different timeline types

Here are the two characteristic timeline types:
The in-time type and the through-time type (also out of time type) are presented and explained with their properties.

In-Time

Timeline passes through the present point (in my body).

  • People go on in here and now (you can enjoy a celebration very well, no plans in mind; you are where you are)
  • Ability to punctuality (overview of the timeline is required) poorly pronounced.(total overload)
  • from the back to the front
  • not all visible before us
  • Time is not so important
  • Memories usually Associated
  • easy to focus on the present

Through time

Timeline is completely outside the person.

  • Western
  • from left to right
  • past, present, future
  • visible before us, clear
  • ordered existence/time schedules more important
  • Memories usually dissociated
  • more difficult to stay in here and now
  • I have paid and I want all the time I have

Some people can change very well, e.g. on holiday. Aging out of now.

Spatial configuration of the timeline

Various spatial arrangements of time lines are possible and are shown here in their peculiarities.

There are very characteristic forms, e.g. timebeuling, time horizon, which are the expression of a specific traumatization.

time horizon

In this type of timeline, the person concerned can no longer visualize future images after this horizon. However, this does not automatically mean that their lives are endangered or insecure after that, although this also happens. Example: Heart attack patient sees only black hole in 3 years. At short times: fear of death.

Time hole

Here the person has a period for which she can hardly or even not make pictures. If you want to meet this person within the next 7 days, you have no problems. But if you want to know if you can go to the theatre together in 14 days, you will get the answer that you can't plan so far into the future, because you don't know if you want to, etc. But if you want to know if this person is willing to go to America in 4 years, you will get a definitive answer.

Temporary

This timeline comes from a man, about 40 years old. He had experienced a divorce three years ago, which still made him strong. When he was asked how the last three years had gone, he could talk about it quite normal. The same was true of his future. But at the moment when he was to talk about something that is three years in the future, he became depressed and expressed himself hopeless. However, his mood changed immediately when you went on to the future. Apparently his mourning for the past influenced his future ideas.

In addition to bumps or holes, the "Adjustment angle" Your timeline is important. This means the angle your timeline has to the horizontal. Your timeline can be horizontal, rising or falling.

Languages

The timeline types and the way we represent time are represented by our language and expressions. Using the vocabulary used, we can draw first conclusions on the timeline.

Welchen Unterschied kannst Du in Deiner Reaktion auf die folgenden Formulierungen feststellen?

  • Denke an eine Zeit, als Du gerade beim Wandern warst.
  • Denke an das letzte Mal, als Du eine Wanderung gemacht hast.
  • Remember how you're about to walk.
  • Denke an eine Zeit, als Du eine Wanderung machen wirst.

And now I invite you to be in the distant future and an experience from the past to rethink that has not yet taken place:

Denke an einen Zeitpunkt, an dem Du eine Wanderung gemacht haben wirst.

1. The:
"So you were afraid? Is that what you felt?"

Two. The:
"So you're scared? What keeps you afraid?"

We constantly influence each other and others with such language patterns. Let's get it, what we want?

Redemptions

  • That's behind me!
  • That's before me!
  • Time's gone.
  • Looking back.
  • There's a lot more to me.
  • The past catches me.
  • I'll leave it to the left, etc.
  • Time is on my side

Removal of bumps and holes

Here is how to show timelines of Holes and freed beuling.

  1. Imagine your timelines are like a linen line and the individual images are attached to this line; then imagine how someone pulls on that leash, so that the bumps disappear.
  2. Imagine how you would like to have your timelines and ask someone to keep his hand where you want the picture. Imagine the respective picture there.

    Make your timelines aesthetic, e.g. straight or parabola, without bumping and overlapping.

Design criteria


  1. Future and past should be clearly differentiated: e.g. front-to-back, left-right

  2. If the lines cross, it'll be sick.

  3. It is unfavorable when timelines turn around so that they can be confused.

The submodalities of time

Discover the peculiarities of their timeline. For some, this is one of the most impressive sections in their NLP training.

Tempting future

The format "stripping future" is a technique with which you can plan your future and pre-programm it on the timeline.

Re-Imprinting after Dilts

One of the most effective NLP formats. It is suitable, among other things, for the treatment of serious trauma and worse experiences in early childhood.
