Gunther Schmidt & the hypnosystemic therapy

Gunther Schmidt (*1943) is an important German psychotherapist, social scientist and pioneer of systemic therapy and consulting. With his innovative combination of systemic therapy, hypnotherapy and solution-oriented approaches, he has significantly influenced modern psychotherapy and coaching landscape. Its hypnosystemic approach combines deep psychological insights with systemic view and places particular emphasis on resources,

Childhood and Youth

There are no extensive biographical details publicly known about Gunther Schmidt's early years and family background. Born in 1943, he grew up in Germany and early began his interest in social sciences, psychology and philosophy, which should later shape his scientific and therapeutic work.

Education and academic career

Gunther Schmidt studied economics first in the 1960s, then medicine at Heidelberg University. He continued his academic career at the University of Heidelberg, where he also worked as a professor and trainer from the 1970s. He was involved in the development of systemic training programs and was involved in research and teaching until the 1990s.

In parallel, he completed various family therapeutic and systemic trainings, including trainings in hypnotherapy according to Milton Erickson in the USA (1979), in psychodrama, further training in transaction analysis, form therapy and various body therapies.

He had particular importance in combining and integrating these methods to reflect the diverse facets of human communication and change.

He worked as a professor and trainer for many years, including at Heidelberg University. He was part of the Heidelberg School, which contributed significantly to the development of systemic therapy in Germany with the founded Heidelberg family therapy.

Career and development of hypnosystemic therapy

Gunther Schmidt is one of the pioneers who combined systemic therapy with hypnotherapy and thus founder of the so-called hypnosystemic approach or hypnosystemic. This approach assumes that people can organize themselves within social systems -- family, profession, society -- and be influenced by communication and relationship patterns. Change takes place by activating resources and consciously controlling states of consciousness, in particular by trance and hypnosis.

Schmidt realized early that conventional forms of therapy often focus too much on problems while propagating a resource-oriented, solution-focused attitude. His work is characterized by the connection of:

  • System theory (e.g. after Bateson and Watzlawick)
  • Hypnotherapy (influenced by Milton Erickson)
  • Communication theory
  • Resource activation

out.

His methods include:

  • Hypnosystemic therapy: combination of systemic vision and hypnotic methods
  • Systemic structural setups: Extension of classical family setup to systemic and hypnotherapeutic principles
  • Solution-oriented short-term therapy: Focus on rapid activation of change potentials

He is co-founder of the Deutsche Gesellschaft für Systemische Therapie, Beratung und Familientherapie (DGSF), which contributes significantly to the professionalisation and dissemination of systemic work in Germany. As well as the IGST (International Society for Systemic Therapy) together with Helm Stierlin, Fritz B. Simon, Gunthard Weber and others.

In 2004 he was also one of the founding members of the Deutsche Bundesverband Coaching (DBVC) and is responsible for the coaching curricula certified by the DBVC for hypnosystemic therapy.

Working and research priorities

The idea of combining systemic and hypnotherapeutic concepts into an overall concept in the sense of a systemically competent holistic psychotherapy is the main focus of Gunther Schmidt's work. He sees his work not only as therapy, but as a contribution to psychosomatic health development -- in the sense of resource activation, self-effectiveness and sustainable change. In the medical and pychosocial field, the therapy of psychosis, forced, depression and severe psychosomatic problems, eating disorders (Bulimie, Anorexie), addiction problems, phobias, couple and sexual problems form a research focus. Furthermore, a focus is on hypnosystemic organisational consulting, team development, coaching and personality development.

Effect and influence

Gunther Schmidt's work has gained meaning far beyond Germany's borders. His concepts and methods are applied in various fields, including:

The hypnosystemic approach according to Gunther Schmidt is now used in many areas:

  • Psychotherapy &amp; Consulting
    Especially in work with resources, trauma therapy, psychosomatic complaints and solution-oriented approaches.
  • Coaching & Personality Development
    To promote self-organisation, decision-making and internal clarity among individuals and teams.
  • Management Development & Organisational Consulting
    In complex working environments to support constructive communication, role clarification and self-management.
  • Healthcare & psychosomatics
    Especially when working with doctors, clinics and psychosomatic diseases -- e.g. at the Health Centre Bodensee.
  • Training
    In zahlreichen Seminaren, Kongressen und an renommierten Weiterbildungsinstituten, etwa als Leiter des Milton-Erickson-Instituts Heidelberg, dem Helm-Stierlin-Instituts in Heidelberg oder internationalen Kongressen.

The approach is also constantly evolving -- also by colleagues from therapy, neurobiology, organizational psychology and coaching. as recognition for his work of life in the field of therapy, coaching and hypnosystemic work, he received the Life Achievement Award of the continuing education industry, one of the highest awards in the German continuing education landscape

News

Gunther Schmidt continues to offer systemic therapy and advice and is considered one of the most famous representatives in Germany. Beyond his long-term clinical leadership and teaching, he remains active as supervisor, lecturer and author. Today he works in two areas: first in the clinical field and second in the field of organizational and team development and in individual coaching. As a specialist in psychosomatic medicine and psychotherapy, he is a medical director of the systelios clinic who uses hypnosystemic trauma therapy. The institute is located at the Hardberg in Wald-Michelbach in the Odenwald, and Gunther Schmidt has designed the structure and therapy concept itself. He also works as a coach and consultant in organizational development, as a lecturer, author and director of the Milton-Erickson-Institut Heidelberg. The Institute of Systemic Hypnotherapy offers training for psychotherapy, coaching and organizational development.

Important publications by Gunther Schmidt on hypnotherapy and co.

1. “Lovely between problem and solution. Hypnosystemic work in difficult contexts" (2004)

In this book, Schmidt describes how problematic behaviors have emerged as solutions in specific contexts and how this perspective can be used in therapy. It combines systemic and hypnotherapeutic approaches to open up new possibilities for action for clients.

Two. "Introduction into hypnosystemic therapy and advice" (2005) Carl-Auer Compact

This work offers a compact and practical introduction to the hypnosystemic approach. Schmidt explains the basics, peculiarities and application areas of this method, which combines systemic therapy with hypnotherapy. The book is aimed at therapists and consultants who want to make their work resource-oriented and solution-focused.

3. "Good advice in the crisis" (2010)

Together with Anna Dollinger and Björn Müller-Kalthoff, this book provides insights into the advice of managers and organisations in crisis situations. Systemic and hypnosystemic concepts are presented that help overcome challenges and make changes successful.

4. “Reden is not enough? Bifocal multi-sensory intervention strategies for therapy and consulting" (2016)

In this book, which Schmidt wrote together with Michael Bohne, Matthias Ohler and Bernhard Trenkle, innovative intervention strategies are presented that go beyond verbal communication. Multisensory approaches are described which enrich and make therapeutic work more effective.

Five. "Alcohol dependency (systemic treatment)" (2017)

Together with Rudolf Klein, Schmidt offers a structural approach to the treatment of alcohol dependence in this book. Concepts and methods are presented that help to understand the complex relationships of addiction behavior and develop effective interventions
